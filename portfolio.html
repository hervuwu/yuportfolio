Short Film: PAGANDUY

As the Director, Editor, and Sound Designer for Paganduy, my goal was to translate the internal struggle of a college student into a visceral, narrative-driven experience. Structured as a flashback, the film relies heavily on dialogue and introspective narration to guide the audience through the protagonist's academic and mental fatigue. By pairing these vocal performances with claustrophobic framing, dynamic editing, and custom foley, the film creates an immersive exploration of the heavy cost of ambition.

Short Film: The Best Jollibee of All Time

Wearing multiple hats as the Writer, Director, and Lead Editor allowed me to craft this post-modernist comedy directly from script to final render. The film follows a stubbornly ambitious (and ultimately doomed) daytime quest for the perfect Jollibee meal. Due to runtime constraints, I utilized elliptical editing to bounce the characters erratically between different branches, using the cut itself to imply the exhausting passage of time and physical toll. As the story transitions into its heartfelt ending—revealing that the day was really about their bond, not the food—the natural shift from day to night provides a stark, grounding visual resolution to their chaotic journey.

Short Film: UNO

UNO was an exciting exercise in stylistic filmmaking. By taking a modern, frustrating experience—losing a game of UNO due to factors completely out of your control—and placing it in the world of early 1920s silent cinema, the film creates a unique juxtaposition. Managing the full production lifecycle allowed me to experiment heavily with vintage visual treatments, exaggerated pacing, and expressive, physical acting. Since the film is completely silent with no dialogue or sound effects, the comedic timing and narrative tension were driven entirely by visual cues, precise editing, and classic slapstick performances.

"BIZNIZ TALK: TALKSHOW"

As the Lead Editor, Motion Graphics Artist, and Audio Engineer for this project, I was tasked with converting a standard discussion on entrepreneurship into a dynamic talk show experience. Since I was not involved in the original filmmaking, I faced the significant challenge of working with poor-quality source audio; I utilized specialized restoration techniques to clean and normalize the tracks, ensuring the final output met professional standards. To complete the aesthetic, I drew inspiration from the visual language of Japanese and Korean variety shows, implementing reactive typography and graphical "commentary" to keep the pacing high and the complex subject matter engaging for a digital audience.
